# PROFILE — <your name>

**This is the shape of your profile, not the profile itself. Your profile is a row on your own task
list, labelled `cos-profile`** — the answers live in that row's description. This page exists only
so you can read the questions before you're asked them.

**It is a row and not a file on purpose.** A file is reachable only from the computer holding it, so
it would pin the whole system to one machine — and the run that needs your profile most is the 7am
one that fires while you're asleep, from somewhere that has no filesystem at all. Your list is the
engine's memory; your profile is part of that memory. Delete the row and nothing about you is left.

It holds the personal facts the engine needs to place work where your actual body and actual life
can do it.

**Be precise about what that does and does not mean, because the comfortable version is a lie.** Your
profile **is not packaged** with the plugin and is **never shared** with whoever gave it to you. It
**does** pass through the model on every run — exactly like your mail and your calendar already do,
under your AI provider's terms. **"Not packaged" is not "never leaves your machine,"** and you should
hold us to the difference.

**So apply one test to every line: would you send this fact through a cloud service?** If no,
leave it out. **The engine runs fine without any single field** — a blank line costs you a little
precision; a fact you regret costs you more. Nothing here is mandatory except *your own work*
(below), and even that you can decline.

**Why you are kept out of the law:** the law is universal and portable; *you* are not. Keeping you
out of the law is what lets the law be shared, audited and improved by strangers, and keeping the law
out of you is what lets your facts be corrected without editing a rulebook.

**Fill this in with the setup skill rather than alone** — it will interview you. Handing yourself a
blank template is how the most important section below ends up empty, and an empty one fails
invisibly.

**Keep it to durable facts.** If a line would go stale in six months, it belongs on a task row's
description instead, beside the loop it governs — not here. *(Test: could this become wrong
tomorrow without anyone touching this profile? Then it doesn't belong here.)*

## Your own work — the most important section, and the one nothing else can supply

**The work that is yours, that you actually want, that has nobody chasing it.** The venture. The
book. The portfolio. The thing you'd say you're really trying to do.

**Why this section exists and why it can't be inferred:** the engine's main filter only admits
things where someone is waiting on you and a date breaks. Your life's work has neither. **If you
leave this blank, the engine physically cannot see your own work**, and it will hand you a clean,
competent card every morning composed entirely of other people's demands — and you will not
notice, because it will look like it's working.

**You declare these. The engine never guesses.** Guessing what matters in your life is the one
thing it has no standing to do.

**The questions setup will ask, in this order:**

1. **"Picture us talking a year from today and you're disappointed. What didn't move?"** *(This gets
   the real answer. "What are your goals" gets a LinkedIn answer.)*
2. **"Is anyone chasing you on that?"** — **the discriminator.** If someone is waiting, it's an
   obligation and the first door already catches it. If nobody is waiting and nothing breaks on a
   date, that's *your own work* — and it's exactly the thing that would otherwise be invisible.
3. **"Say what it is in one line."** Not a task list. The engine never decomposes it; you author the
   work. It just protects the room.
4. **"What does it need to stay alive — some push most days, or one real block a week?"** A cadence,
   not an aspiration. The cadence is what the dark-day count is measured against.
5. **"What does a good day on it look like — what would you have actually finished?"** This is how
   the engine recognises it moved.
